The Story of Baijiu

Discover the deep cultural roots of China’s most traditional spirit.

What is Baijiu?

Baijiu (白酒), meaning "white alcohol," is a potent Chinese spirit made primarily from sorghum. With an ABV typically ranging from 40-60%, it's celebrated for its bold flavors and complex production process.

Its Cultural Significance

Baijiu is deeply woven into Chinese tradition. It’s served during celebrations, ceremonies, and significant life events — symbolizing respect, joy, and unity.

How It's Made

The process begins with fermenting grains in earthen pits using a unique microbial starter called qu. Each region in China produces a distinct style, from the strong aroma of Luzhou Laojiao to the light floral notes of Fenjiu.
